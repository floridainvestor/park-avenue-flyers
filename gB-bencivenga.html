A Certificate of Title — Not a Deed. Here Is Why That Matters to You.

In Florida, manufactured homes transfer ownership through a Certificate of Title — not a real property deed. This is standard under Florida law [FL Statute §319.21]. Your Certificate of Title is filed with the Florida Department of Highway Safety and Motor Vehicles (DHSMV). It works like a car title: it is a legal ownership document with your name on it. This matters to you for one reason above all others: it is not a lease. It is not a rental option. It is not a contract that expires. From the day you sign, your name is on that title. The owner cannot take it back because you missed one payment without a formal legal process. Your ownership is documented, filed with the state, and enforceable. Month 59, the title is clear. That document is yours, free and clear, with no further payments ever.

Florida Statute §319.21 — Certificate of Title for Motor Vehicles and Mobile Homes. Terms confirmed in writing at signing.
Here is exactly how this Rent-to-Own works: On the day you sign the agreement, a Certificate of Title is prepared with your name on it — not a lease, not a rental option, a legal title document filed with the state of Florida under Statute §319.21.
